Open Ring is a diagnostic framework for identifying where structures — markets, technologies, practices, institutions — have their fixed and variable elements incorrectly arranged, and what the minimum intervention is to correct that.

The name comes from the function: a compass does not tell you the terrain is easy or how far you have to walk. It tells you whether you are facing the right direction.

A thing only tells you the truth when lying about it costs more than it is worth.

Capture happens when what is locked in place serves the people running a system, and the cost of that lockdown falls on the people using it. It does not require bad actors — only misaligned incentives. The people who would lose from change are usually the same people who control the conditions that prevent it. That is sufficient to keep broken arrangements stable for a very long time.

A locked arrangement stays locked through four mechanisms: Knowledge (the better option is not known, because those who would lose from change control what gets taught), Access (the better option cannot be reached, because distribution runs through controlled channels), Money (the better option cannot be funded, because capital is allocated by those who benefit from the status quo), and Authority (the better option lacks legitimacy, because certification belongs to incumbents).

The diagnostic checks all four.
